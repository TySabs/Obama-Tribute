President Barack Obama

“Change will not come if we wait for some other person or some other time. We are the ones we have been waiting for. We are the change that we seek.”

44th U.S. President - Term: 2009-2017

Here is a timeline of Barack Obama's major accomplishments as president:

  • February 17, 2009: Signs the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 in an effort to recover from the deepening worldwide recession.
  • May 26, 2009: Nominates Sonia Sotomayor to the Supreme Court, becoming the first Hispanic in the Supreme Court.
  • December 10, 2009: Receives Nobel Peace Prize for “extraordinary efforts to strengthen international diplomacy and cooperation between peoples”.
  • March 23, 2010: Signs into law the Affordable Care Act (ACA). The ACA provides the path for over 18 million uninsured Americans getting coverage and revolutionizes healthcare by fixing loopholes such as “pre-existing conditions”.
  • May 10, 2010: Nominates Elena Kagan to the Supreme Court, bringing the total number of women on the Supreme Court to three for the first time in history.
  • March 18, 2011: Authorizes the establishment of a no-fly-zone over Libya in response to Muammar Gaddafi's prolonged mass shelling of his own citizens. Six days later, Obama gives NATO control of the operation.
  • May 1, 2011: US Navy Seals successfully kill al-Qaeda leader, Osama Bin Laden. Six weeks earlier, President Obama rejected a plan to bomb the compound, instead favoring a “surgical raid”.
  • May 9, 2012: Publicly supports the legalization of same-sex marriage, becoming the first US president to do so.
  • January 16, 2013: Signs 23 executive orders and outlines a series of sweeping proposals in an attempt to reform gun control and prevent mass shootings.
  • July 14, 2014: Announces a deal with Iran to see removal of sanctions in exchange for Iran taking comprehensive measures to prevent itself from producing nuclear weapons.
  • November 10, 2014: Recommends the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) reclassify broadband internet service as telecommunications service in order to preserve net neutrality.
  • February 24, 2015: Vetoes a bill that would have authorized the Keystone XL Pipeline, preventing massive amounts of carbon pollution.
  • March 21, 2016: Visits Cuba in an effort to normalize relations between the US and Cuba. Obama is the first president to visit Cuba since Calvin Coolidge in 1928.
  • May 27, 2016: On the 71st anniversary of the atomic bombing of Hiroshima, Obama becomes the first US president to visit Hiroshima, Japan.
